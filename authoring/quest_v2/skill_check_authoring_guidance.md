# Skill Check Dialogue — Authoring Guidance

> This document tells you **how pass and fail text should feel, think, and work** for each skill.
> Do not memorize lines. Internalize the dynamics and generate from them.

---

## Global Rules

**Pass text is always NPC speech.** Never narration, never a tooltip, never "[You succeeded]." The NPC is talking. They are revealing something they would not have volunteered unprompted. The player's skill created the *conditions* for that reveal — the text is the NPC *responding to those conditions*.

**Fail text is always NPC speech.** The NPC chose not to share. They deflected, went vague, or shut the door politely. The player should feel like the *NPC* made a decision, not like a *system* rejected them. No fail text should make the NPC sound aware that a game mechanic occurred.

**Pass text must reveal a deeper layer.** Every pass unlocks something: a fear, a motive, a tactical detail, a personal stake, a reframing of the quest. If the pass text could have been the NPC's default dialogue, it's not a pass — it's just dialogue.

**Fail text must still feel like a conversation.** The player walks away thinking "they didn't want to tell me" — not "I got the wrong answer." Fail text can be warm, cold, cagey, or dismissive, but it is always *a person choosing not to go deeper*.

## CHA Skills

### PERSUASION

**Emotional dynamic:** Trust was earned. The NPC made a *decision* to be vulnerable. They feel safe enough, or respected enough, or heard enough, to cross a line they normally hold. There's a small emotional risk on the NPC's side — they're choosing honesty over self-protection, and they know it.

**Mental state of the NPC (pass):** "This person actually cares. I don't usually do this, but I'm going to be honest." There may be a moment of hesitation before the reveal. The NPC is *choosing* to lower a wall, not having it torn down.

**Logical structure:** The player said or did something that signaled genuine concern, shared values, or trustworthiness. The NPC reciprocates with honesty. Cause → effect is: *demonstrated sincerity* → *earned candor*.

**What pass text is NOT:** It is not the NPC being tricked, pressured, or impressed. If the NPC doesn't realize they're sharing more than intended, that's DECEPTION. If they're sharing because they're scared, that's INTIMIDATION. If they're sharing because the player was charming or entertaining, that's PERFORMANCE.

**Fail dynamic:** The NPC considered opening up but decided not to. They're not hostile — they're just not ready. The door isn't locked, it's just closed. They may acknowledge the player's good intentions while still declining.

---

### DECEPTION

**Emotional dynamic:** The NPC was *played* and doesn't fully realize it. The player used misdirection, a leading question, a casual bluff, or a false assumption that the NPC corrected — accidentally revealing the truth in the process. The emotional note is the NPC's *unguarded moment*: they spoke freely because they didn't perceive a threat. If there's a realization, it comes *after* the reveal, as a flicker of "wait, why did I just say that?"

**Mental state of the NPC (pass):** "Oh, you already know? Then I might as well—" or "Ha, sure, it's not a big deal—" or "Wait. I didn't mean to..." The NPC *felt comfortable* when they shouldn't have. The player created a *false sense of safety or shared knowledge* that lowered the NPC's guard.

**Logical structure:** The player's words implied they already knew something, or reframed the topic so the sensitive part seemed harmless to mention, or asked a question whose honest answer accidentally includes the secret. Cause → effect is: *manufactured comfort or misdirection* → *unintended disclosure*.

**Critical distinction:** The NPC is not "caught." They are not "seen through." They are not admitting something because they were confronted. If the pass text reads like the NPC was *exposed*, it's INSIGHT, not DECEPTION. DECEPTION pass text should feel like a magic trick — the NPC handed over the card and didn't realize it was the one you wanted.

**Fail dynamic:** The NPC smelled the manipulation. They got suspicious, cagey, or annoyed. They don't necessarily know *what* the player was after, but they felt the conversational ground shift and pulled back. The energy is "nice try" — not "you failed to perceive."

---

### INTIMIDATION

**Emotional dynamic:** Fear, or a cold calculation that silence costs more than speech. The NPC is not *choosing* to trust the player — they're *choosing survival*. There's resentment, resignation, or panic underneath the disclosure. The NPC is giving up something they were *protecting*, and they're not happy about it.

**Mental state of the NPC (pass):** "I'd rather deal with you knowing than deal with what happens if I don't talk." The NPC has done a rapid cost-benefit analysis, and the player is the more immediate threat. They may be angry, scared, or grimly practical, but they are *not* warming up to the player.

**Logical structure:** The player made the consequences of silence tangible — through words, presence, or reputation. The NPC decided that the risk of sharing is lower than the risk of silence. Cause → effect is: *credible threat or overwhelming pressure* → *reluctant disclosure to neutralize the threat*.

**What pass text is NOT:** It is not grudging respect (that's PERSUASION succeeding through force of character). It is not the NPC being impressed (that's PERFORMANCE). Intimidation pass text should never feel *friendly*. The NPC may cooperate, but they don't *like* it.

**Fail dynamic:** The NPC refused to break. They're tougher, more stubborn, or less afraid than the player expected. They may be offended, defiant, or coldly dismissive. The energy is "that doesn't work on me" — delivered with confidence, not with narrator framing.

---

### PERFORMANCE

**Emotional dynamic:** The player's *presence* shifted the room. Showmanship, charisma, humor, a well-told story, a dramatic gesture — the NPC was moved, entertained, or socially disarmed. They share more freely because the player created a *vibe* where openness feels natural, even fun. The emotional note is warmth, camaraderie, or being swept up in a moment.

**Mental state of the NPC (pass):** "I like this person. They've got something. I want to match their energy." The NPC isn't *deciding* to trust — they're *feeling* generous. The reveal comes easily because the mood is right, not because a logical argument was made.

**Logical structure:** The player demonstrated social magnetism, and the NPC responded to it emotionally. Cause → effect is: *impressive or disarming social display* → *NPC feels drawn in and shares more freely than usual*.

**Critical distinction from PERSUASION:** Persuasion is "you earned my trust with sincerity." Performance is "you charmed me and I got caught up in it." Persuasion NPCs made a *deliberate choice*. Performance NPCs got *swept along*. If the pass text involves the NPC carefully deciding to be honest, it's PERSUASION. If the NPC is grinning, laughing, or vibing when they share, it's PERFORMANCE.

**Fail dynamic:** The NPC wasn't impressed, or the showmanship felt forced, or they're just not in the mood. They don't punish the attempt — they just don't reward it. Mild indifference or gentle deflection, not hostility.

## WIS Skills

### INSIGHT

**Emotional dynamic:** The player *saw* the NPC — saw through the front, the deflection, the brave face. The NPC feels *exposed* but also, possibly, *relieved*. Someone finally noticed what they've been carrying. The emotional note is vulnerability — the NPC's mask slipped or was gently lifted, and now they're deciding whether to keep pretending.

**Mental state of the NPC (pass):** "…You can tell? I thought I was hiding it." The NPC is surprised that their interior state was visible. There may be defensiveness that quickly softens into honesty, or an exhale of relief. The NPC was *hiding something emotional* — a fear, a grief, a conflict of loyalty, a secret motivation — and the player named it or got close enough that denial feels pointless.

**Logical structure:** The player read body language, tone, subtext, or emotional inconsistency. The NPC's words said one thing but their manner said another, and the player called it. Cause → effect is: *accurate emotional read* → *NPC drops the pretense on something personal*.

**Critical distinction from DECEPTION:** INSIGHT is "I see what you're hiding." DECEPTION is "I just got you to show me without realizing it." If the NPC feels *seen*, it's INSIGHT. If the NPC feels *comfortable* (and shouldn't), it's DECEPTION. Insight NPCs know they were read. Deception NPCs may not realize anything happened.

**Critical distinction from INVESTIGATION:** INSIGHT reads *people*. INVESTIGATION reads *information*. If the gap is emotional (the NPC is scared but pretending to be fine), it's INSIGHT. If the gap is logical (the NPC's timeline doesn't add up), it's INVESTIGATION.

**Fail dynamic:** The NPC maintains the front. They may brush off the attempt warmly ("I'm fine, really"), dismissively ("you're overthinking it"), or defensively ("that's a bit of a reach"). The wall holds.

---

### PERCEPTION

**Emotional dynamic:** The player noticed something *physical* that the NPC overlooked, downplayed, or assumed nobody would see. A mark, a track, a damaged object, something out of place. The NPC is surprised — not emotionally exposed, but caught off guard that the detail was visible. The emotional note is "oh — you saw that?"

**Mental state of the NPC (pass):** "I didn't think that was noticeable" or "I forgot about that" or "I was hoping nobody would look there." The NPC isn't guarding a feeling — they're guarding (or ignoring) a *physical fact*. The player's sharp senses forced a detail into the conversation that the NPC wouldn't have raised.

**Logical structure:** The player observed something concrete and specific in the environment, on the NPC, or in the physical situation. The NPC now has to address it. Cause → effect is: *sharp sensory observation* → *NPC acknowledges a physical detail and its implications*.

**Critical distinction from INVESTIGATION:** PERCEPTION notices. INVESTIGATION connects. If the player *saw* the scratches on the door, that's PERCEPTION. If the player *asked why the scratches don't match the story*, that's INVESTIGATION. Perception is the eye. Investigation is the follow-up question.

**Fail dynamic:** The NPC genuinely doesn't know what the player is referring to, or dismisses the observation as unremarkable. "Looks normal to me." No emotional deflection — just a factual shrug.

## INT Skills

### INVESTIGATION

**Emotional dynamic:** The player found a *seam* in the story — a gap, a contradiction, a missing piece — and pulled on it. The NPC is confronted with the fact that their account doesn't hold up under scrutiny. The emotional note is being caught in an *incomplete* story, not necessarily a *dishonest* one. The NPC may have simplified, omitted, or genuinely not realized the inconsistency.

**Mental state of the NPC (pass):** "You're right — those two things don't add up. The reason is..." or "I left that part out — I didn't think anyone would catch it." The NPC is responding to a *logical challenge*, not an emotional one. They're filling a gap because the player demonstrated it exists.

**Logical structure:** The player cross-referenced details, noticed a timeline problem, identified a missing actor in the story, or asked a question that revealed an assumption the NPC was making. Cause → effect is: *logical pressure on a weak point in the narrative* → *NPC provides the missing piece*.

**Critical distinction from INSIGHT:** Investigation finds gaps in *information*. Insight finds gaps in *emotion*. "Your story doesn't add up" is INVESTIGATION. "You seem scared of something you're not mentioning" is INSIGHT.

**Fail dynamic:** The NPC can't or won't elaborate. They may genuinely not know the answer, or they may not see the inconsistency. "That's all I know" or "I told you everything." Flat, factual, no emotional charge.

### NATURE

**Emotional dynamic:** The player demonstrated *practical expertise* — knowledge of creature behavior, terrain, weather, seasons, plants, survival. The NPC respects it. They've been dumbing things down or skipping the technical truth because they assumed the player wouldn't understand. Now they don't have to. The emotional note is *relief at talking to a peer*, or *respect for someone who knows the land*.

**Mental state of the NPC (pass):** "Finally, someone who gets it." The NPC shifts registers — they stop giving the tourist version and start giving the real one. They share details they'd normally skip because most people wouldn't grasp the significance.

**Logical structure:** The player demonstrated domain knowledge that signaled competence. The NPC reciprocated by upgrading the quality of information. Cause → effect is: *demonstrated expertise about the natural world* → *NPC shares practitioner-level detail they'd normally withhold*.

**Fail dynamic:** The NPC stays in "explaining to a city person" mode. They simplify, generalize, or say "you'd have to see it to understand." No condescension necessary — just a gap in shared knowledge that keeps the conversation surface-level.

---

### HISTORY

**Emotional dynamic:** The player recognized a pattern, a precedent, or a connection to past events that the NPC assumed was forgotten. The NPC is *surprised and gratified* that someone remembers. The emotional note is the loneliness of carrying old knowledge suddenly being broken — someone *else* knows.

**Mental state of the NPC (pass):** "You know about that? I stopped trying to explain it to people years ago." The NPC had given up on this knowledge being relevant to anyone else. The player's recognition opens a door the NPC had closed.

**Logical structure:** The player connected the present situation to historical precedent. The NPC, seeing that the player has the foundation, builds on it with the part of the story that requires context to understand. Cause → effect is: *demonstrated historical knowledge* → *NPC shares the contextual layer that depends on that knowledge*.

**Fail dynamic:** The NPC doesn't see the point of explaining the history. "It was a long time ago" or "that doesn't really matter now." They're not hostile — they just don't think the player has the framework to receive the information.

---

### ARCANA

**Emotional dynamic:** The player recognized something *wrong* — something that doesn't obey ordinary rules. The NPC has been *afraid to say it out loud* because it sounds insane, or because they don't have the vocabulary for it. The emotional note is *validation and relief*: someone else sees it, someone else has words for it. The NPC may have been doubting their own perception.

**Mental state of the NPC (pass):** "You can tell? Thank the gods — I thought I was imagining it." The NPC has been isolated with strange knowledge. The player's recognition breaks that isolation. They share eagerly, urgently, or with the relief of someone who's been carrying something alone.

**Logical structure:** The player identified arcane, magical, or supernaturally significant elements that the NPC noticed but couldn't contextualize. The NPC, validated, shares the full scope of what they've observed. Cause → effect is: *recognized the unnatural element* → *NPC shares everything they've been too afraid or unequipped to articulate*.

**Fail dynamic:** The NPC keeps the strange observations to themselves. "If there's something weird going on, it's beyond me." They may *want* to talk about it but don't trust that the player will take it seriously, or they may genuinely not have noticed the arcane dimension.

---

### RELIGION

**Emotional dynamic:** The player understood something *sacred* — a custom, a belief, a spiritual practice — that the NPC had assumed outsiders would mock, ignore, or trivialize. The NPC feels *seen in a way they'd given up on*. The emotional note is the particular relief of having one's faith or traditions treated with genuine understanding by a stranger.

**Mental state of the NPC (pass):** "You actually understand. I stopped trying to explain this years ago." The NPC has built a wall between their inner spiritual world and the people they interact with daily. The player just walked through it respectfully. The NPC opens up about the *sacred significance* they normally keep private.

**Logical structure:** The player demonstrated knowledge of or respect for a belief system, ritual, tradition, or spiritual framework. The NPC, recognizing a kindred understanding, shares the deeper religious or cultural context. Cause → effect is: *demonstrated spiritual/cultural literacy* → *NPC shares the sacred layer they protect from outsiders*.

**Critical note:** The NPC is not *educating* the player from scratch. They're sharing *deeper material* because the player already has the foundation. If the pass text reads like a lecture or a lore entry, it's wrong — it should feel like a conversation between people who share a frame of reference.

**Fail dynamic:** The NPC politely declines to discuss sacred matters. "It's tradition — I wouldn't expect an outsider to understand." There's no resentment, just a firm boundary between the sacred and the public. The NPC may even be warm about it — "no offense" energy, not hostility.

## Skill Confusion Quick-Reference

| If the NPC feels... | The skill is probably... |
|---|---|
| Safe enough to be honest | PERSUASION |
| Unaware they revealed something | DECEPTION |
| Cornered or calculating survival | INTIMIDATION |
| Swept up, entertained, charmed | PERFORMANCE |
| Emotionally exposed / seen | INSIGHT |
| Surprised you noticed a physical detail | PERCEPTION |
| Confronted with a gap in their story | INVESTIGATION |
| Respected for practical expertise | NATURE |
| Surprised anyone remembers | HISTORY |
| Relieved someone else sees the strangeness | ARCANA |
| Moved that an outsider respects their faith | RELIGION |

## Anti-Patterns

**Do not write pass text where the NPC narrates the mechanic.**
- ✗ "Your words are persuasive."
- ✗ "You make a compelling argument."
- These are *descriptions of persuasion*, not *responses to being persuaded*.

**Do not write pass text that could be default dialogue.**
- If the NPC would say it without any skill check, it's not a pass. The pass must unlock a *new layer*.

**Do not write fail text that sounds like a system message.**
- ✗ "I don't have anything else to tell you." (feels like a dialogue tree dead end)
- ✓ "I've said my piece. Take it or leave it." (feels like a person making a choice)

**Do not let the emotional tone of pass text drift across skills.**
- DECEPTION pass should never feel like the NPC was *confronted*.
- INSIGHT pass should never feel like the NPC was *tricked*.
- PERSUASION pass should never feel like the NPC was *pressured*.
- INTIMIDATION pass should never feel like the NPC *chose freely*.
- If you can swap the skill label and the text still reads correctly, the text is too generic.
